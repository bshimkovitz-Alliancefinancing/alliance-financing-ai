Types of Acquisitions We Finance

  • Complete Business Purchases: Buy 100% of an operating business with established cash flow
  • Partial Acquisitions: Purchase majority or minority stakes in existing businesses
  • Management Buyouts (MBO): Existing management team purchases the business from current owners
  • Mergers & Acquisitions: Combine two businesses or acquire competitors
  • Partner Buyouts: Buy out co-owners or partners to gain full control
  • Franchise Purchases: Finance the acquisition of franchise businesses

Financing Options

SBA 7(a) Loans (U.S.): Government-backed loans up to $5M with as little as 10% down. Favorable terms for qualified buyers. Best for businesses under $10M purchase price.

CSBFL - Canada Small Business Financing Loan (Canada): Government-backed program for Canadian businesses to finance up to $1M for business acquisitions and equipment. Favorable terms with lower down payments and longer amortization periods for qualified Canadian borrowers.

Conventional Bank Loans: Traditional acquisition financing for larger deals ($5M+). Typically require 20-30% down and strong buyer credentials.

Seller Financing: Seller provides part of financing, showing confidence in business. Often combined with bank loans to reduce cash needed upfront.

Asset-Based Lending: Secured by business assets (inventory, equipment, receivables). Good for asset-heavy businesses.

Who Qualifies?

  • Personal credit score of 680+ (some SBA programs accept 650+)
  • Industry experience or relevant business management background
  • 10-30% of purchase price for down payment and working capital
  • Target business shows consistent profitability (3+ years preferred)
  • Strong cash flow to service debt (1.25x DSCR minimum)
  • Clean personal and business financial history

What Lenders Evaluate

Target Business Performance: 3-5 years of financial statements, tax returns, and profit trends. Consistent profitability is key.

Buyer Qualifications: Your industry experience, management skills, and financial strength matter as much as the business itself.

Business Valuation: Independent valuation ensures fair purchase price (typically 2-4x annual earnings for small businesses).

Transition Plan: Lenders want to see a solid plan for taking over operations and maintaining performance.

The Acquisition Process

1. Find the Right Business: Identify acquisition targets through brokers, direct outreach, or our network.

2. Due Diligence: Review financials, operations, legal issues, and customer base. Our lenders can recommend advisors.

3. Business Valuation: Independent appraisal determines fair market value and supports financing application.

4. Financing Application: Apply through our platform to get matched with specialized acquisition lenders.

5. Close the Deal: Complete financing, transfer ownership, and begin operations under new ownership.

Benefits of Buying vs. Starting

  • Immediate Cash Flow: Established revenue from day one reduces startup risk
  • Proven Systems: Operations, suppliers, and workflows already in place
  • Existing Customers: Built-in customer base and brand recognition
  • Trained Staff: Experienced employees who know the business
  • Easier Financing: Lenders prefer proven businesses over startups
  • Faster Growth: Expand immediately rather than building from zero

Typical Rates & Terms

SBA 7(a) Rates: Prime + 2.25-2.75% (currently ~11-12%)

Conventional Rates: 7-12% depending on deal size and borrower strength

Loan Terms: 10-25 years for real estate; 5-10 years for business assets

Down Payment: 10-30% depending on loan type and deal structure

Closing Timeline: 60-90 days typical (45 days minimum)

*Rates vary by lender, deal size, borrower strength, and business performance history.
