Auto-Populate Deal Fields

When an application arrives (web form, agent submission, or uploaded docs), AI reads the data and auto-fills the deal profile — company name, industry, revenue, amount, product type.

How it works: Parse form_data JSON from applications_sent table. Extract key fields using pattern matching + NLP. Pre-fill the deal record before an analyst even opens it. Flag fields with low confidence for human review.

Scale impact: At 50 deals/week, saves ~2 hours of manual data entry per day.
⚖️

Deal Risk Scoring

Every deal gets a risk score (0-100) based on credit profile strength, industry risk, deal structure, document completeness, and historical approval patterns.

Scoring factors:
• Credit score vs. lender minimums (weighted 25%)
• Time in business (15%)
• Industry risk tier (15%)
• Deal size vs. typical approval range (15%)
• Document completeness (15%)
• Revenue-to-loan ratio (15%)

Output: 🟢 Low Risk (75-100) | 🟡 Medium Risk (50-74) | 🔴 High Risk (0-49)
Shown as a badge on every deal card and table row.
🏦

Enhanced Lender Matching

Your existing 8-factor lender scoring engine gets upgraded with historical win-rate data — which lenders actually approve deals like this one, not just which ones might.

Current: Scores deals against lender criteria (product, size, credit, geography, etc.)
Upgrade: Layer in historical data — "For deals with this profile, Equitable Bank has approved 78% vs. CIT Capital at 52%." Uses your own funded deal history as training data.

New output: Lender cards ranked by fit score + historical approval rate + estimated timeline + rate range. Side-by-side comparison view.
💡

What AI Extracts — By Document Type

The analysis adapts based on the financing product. An MCA deal gets bank statement stacking analysis. An equipment lease gets asset evaluation. Each product gets a tailored report.

🏦 Bank Statements (3-6 months)
MCA Working Capital CSBFL All Products
Core Metrics Extracted
• Average daily balance (per month)
• Monthly deposits total + count
• Monthly withdrawals total
• Ending balance trend (up/down/flat)
• Highest / lowest balance days
• Revenue deposit patterns (regular vs irregular)
🚩 Red Flags AI Detects
• MCA stacking — existing daily/weekly MCA payments

MCA-Specific: AI identifies all existing MCA/advance payments, calculates total daily/weekly obligation, and estimates remaining balances. This is the #1 thing lenders ask about — and the most tedious to do manually.

How It Works Under the Hood

📄
1. Document Upload
Agent or client uploads docs to the deal. PDFs, images, CSVs — any format. Docs tagged by type (bank stmt, financials, etc.)
🔍
2. AI Extraction
AI reads each document. OCR for images/scanned PDFs. NLP for structured extraction. Numbers, dates, names, patterns — all pulled automatically.
⚖️
3. Cross-Reference
AI compares data across documents. Revenue on app vs deposits in bank. Obligations stated vs payments found. Catches what humans miss.

Underwriting report appears on the deal. Summary, metrics, flags, cross-checks. Analyst reviews, annotates, and moves to lender submission.

Routing Rules Engine

Configurable rules that determine who gets what. Admins set the rules, AI executes them automatically.

Rule examples:
• Equipment deals over $250K → Senior analyst team
• CSBFL products → Sarah Chen (CSBFL specialist)
• Vendor-sourced deals → Vendor program desk
• Agent's repeat client → Same analyst as previous deal
• Mortgage products → Mortgage team

Rules are prioritized: specific rules override general ones. Fallback: round-robin within the matching team.
⚖️

Workload Balancing

AI considers each team member's current pipeline — active deal count, total pipeline value, and deals in critical stages — before assigning.

Balance factors:
• Active deal count (weight: 40%)
• Pipeline value under management (20%)
• Deals in critical stages — In Credit, Docs (20%)
• Availability status — on leave, capacity cap (20%)

Result: No one gets overloaded. New deals flow to team members with capacity. Dashboard shows workload heatmap for admins.
📊

Assignment Queue

A new "Unassigned" column in the Kanban board and a dedicated queue view. Deals waiting for assignment are instantly visible — no deal falls through the cracks.

Queue shows: Deal summary, AI-suggested assignee (with confidence %), time waiting, risk score, and one-click assign button.

Escalation: Unassigned for 2 hours → Command Center alert. 4 hours → auto-assign to next available by round-robin. Admin always notified.
🔄

Reassignment & Handoff

When a team member goes on leave, hits capacity, or a deal needs a specialist — one-click reassignment with full context handoff.

Handoff includes: Activity timeline, all notes, current lender status, pending tasks, and an auto-generated handoff summary so the new assignee doesn't start from zero.

Bulk reassign: Admin can reassign all of a team member's active deals at once (e.g., coverage during vacation).

5 Strategic Gaps to Close

These are the pieces that don't have their own flashy section but will make or break everything above at scale. Each one is a force multiplier — skip them and the AI features underperform; build them and the whole system compounds.

📈

Gap 1: Lender Outcome Tracking

Highest Priority — Start Collecting Now
BLOCKING FOR AI
The AI lender matching in Phase 3 needs historical data: which lenders approved deals like this, how long it took, what rate they gave, why they declined. Without this data, the AI is guessing from criteria alone. Every week you delay is a week of lost training data.
What to Add (Deal Modal)
• Lender decision: Approved / Declined / Conditional
• Decision date (for velocity tracking)
• Approved rate + terms offered
• Decline reason (dropdown: credit, time in business, industry, deal size, docs incomplete, other)
• Days from submission to decision
• Conditions required (if conditional)
How This Pays Off
• AI learns: "For $200K equipment deals, BDC approves 74% in avg 12 days"
• AI warns: "CIT declines 80% of construction deals under 5yr"
• Analysts see historical patterns before choosing a lender
• Pipeline analytics show conversion by lender
• After 100 tracked outcomes, the AI matching accuracy jumps significantly
Implementation: Small change — add 5-6 fields to the lender submission section of the deal modal + a Supabase table lender_decisions (deal_id, lender_id, decision, rate, terms, decline_reason, decision_date). Can be built in one session and starts collecting data immediately.
💬

Gap 2: Communications Tied to Deals

High Priority — Needed for Activity Timeline
BUILDS ON PHASE 1
Right now emails and calls happen outside the deal record. When someone emails a lender about a deal, that context disappears. The activity timeline in Phase 1 only works if everything — notes, status changes, docs, and communications — flows into it.
Email Logging
Emails sent from the dashboard (to lenders, agents, clients) auto-log on the deal timeline with subject, recipient, and timestamp.
Lender Responses
When a lender responds (condition list, approval, decline), log it on the deal with the response details attached.
Agent Updates
Status updates sent to referring agents auto-log. Agent replies or uploaded docs also appear on the timeline.

Gap 5: Compliance & Audit Trail

Medium Priority — Essential for Credibility at Scale
REGULATORY
In financing, you need to show your work. Every AI recommendation, every document analysis, every lender submission decision should be logged permanently. If a regulator, auditor, or lender asks "why did you approve/submit this deal?" — you have a defensible paper trail.
What Gets Logged
• AI underwriting report (snapshot at time of generation)
• Risk score + all contributing factors
• Lender match recommendations + reasoning
• Analyst decisions (accept/override AI recommendations)
• Document analysis findings + red flags
• Every status change with who/when/why
Why It Matters
• Lenders gain confidence: "Alliance shows us their full underwriting"
• Regulatory compliance: complete audit trail for every deal
• Internal accountability: who overrode the AI? Why?
• AI improvement: log when analysts disagree with AI to retrain
• Dispute resolution: exact history of every deal decision
• Professional credibility at scale
