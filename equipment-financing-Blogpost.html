What is Equipment Financing?

Equipment financing is a loan specifically designed to purchase business equipment. You borrow money to buy the equipment outright, and the equipment itself serves as collateral for the loan. Once you've paid off the loan, you own the equipment free and clear.

How Equipment Financing Works:

  • Loan Amount: Typically 80-100% of equipment cost
  • Term Length: 2-7 years, depending on equipment lifespan
  • Ownership: You own the equipment from day one
  • Collateral: The equipment secures the loan
  • End Result: Full ownership with no further payments

What is Equipment Leasing?

Equipment leasing is essentially renting equipment for a specified period. You make monthly payments to use the equipment, but the leasing company retains ownership. At the end of the lease term, you typically have options to purchase, return, or upgrade the equipment.

Types of Equipment Leases:

  • Operating Lease (True Lease): Short-term rental, equipment returned at end
  • Capital Lease (Finance Lease): Longer term, option to purchase at end
  • Fair Market Value (FMV) Lease: Purchase for fair market value at end
  • $1 Buyout Lease: Essentially financing disguised as leasing

Tax Implications for Canadian Businesses

Understanding Canadian tax treatment is crucial when deciding between financing and leasing.

Equipment Financing Tax Benefits:

  • Capital Cost Allowance (CCA): Depreciate equipment value over time (rates vary by equipment class)
  • Interest Deduction: Loan interest is fully tax-deductible
  • Accelerated Investment Incentive: First-year CCA deduction increased to 1.5x in recent years
  • Instant Asset Write-Off: Some equipment may qualify for immediate expense deduction

Equipment Leasing Tax Benefits:

  • Full Deductibility: Entire lease payment is tax-deductible as operating expense
  • GST/HST Treatment: GST/HST on lease payments can be claimed as input tax credit
  • Simplified Accounting: No depreciation calculations required

Tax rules change frequently, and your specific situation (profit level, tax bracket, equipment type) significantly affects which option provides better tax benefits. Always consult with a Canadian tax professional before making your decision.

When Equipment Financing Makes Sense

Choose financing when:

  • You plan to use the equipment for its entire useful life (7+ years)
  • The equipment is essential to your business and unlikely to become obsolete
  • You want to build business equity through asset ownership
  • Your business is profitable and can benefit from CCA deductions
  • You need the equipment as collateral for future financing
  • Long-term cost savings are more important than monthly payment size

Best for: Construction equipment, manufacturing machinery, commercial vehicles, medical equipment with long lifespans

When Equipment Leasing Makes Sense

Choose leasing when:

  • Technology changes rapidly (computers, software, telecom equipment)
  • You need to preserve working capital for growth opportunities
  • Maintaining off-balance-sheet financing helps your financial ratios
  • Equipment maintenance and service are included in lease terms
  • You're testing new equipment before committing to purchase
  • Your business prefers predictable, fixed monthly expenses
  • You want flexibility to upgrade as your needs change

Best for: IT equipment, office technology, short-term project equipment, rapidly evolving machinery

Cash Flow Considerations

Let's look at a real example comparing the two options:

Example: $100,000 Manufacturing Equipment

Financing Option:

  • $100,000 loan at 8% interest over 5 years
  • Monthly payment: ~$2,028
  • Total paid: $121,680
  • You own the equipment

Leasing Option:

  • 5-year FMV lease
  • Monthly payment: ~$1,800
  • Total paid: $108,000
  • Purchase at end: ~$20,000 (fair market value)
  • Total if purchased: $128,000

The Verdict: Financing saves $6,320 over 5 years if you plan to keep the equipment. However, leasing offers $228/month lower payments, freeing up $13,680 in working capital over the term.

Hidden Costs to Consider

Financing Hidden Costs:

  • Maintenance: You're responsible for all repairs
  • Obsolescence Risk: Stuck with outdated equipment
  • Disposal Costs: Selling used equipment takes time and effort
  • Insurance: Must maintain comprehensive coverage

Leasing Hidden Costs:

  • Excess Usage Fees: Charges for exceeding hour/mileage limits
  • Damage Charges: Penalties for wear beyond "normal use"
  • Early Termination: Expensive penalties for ending lease early
  • End-of-Lease Fees: Return fees, inspection costs

Making Your Decision

Answer these questions to guide your choice:

  1. How long will you use this equipment? Longer use ‚Üí Financing wins
  2. How quickly does this technology become obsolete? Fast change ‚Üí Leasing wins
  3. What's your current cash flow situation? Tight cash ‚Üí Leasing helps
  4. Do you need this equipment as collateral? Yes ‚Üí Financing required
  5. What's your effective tax rate? Higher rate ‚Üí Leasing benefits increase
  6. Is equipment maintenance included in lease? Yes ‚Üí Leasing value increases

Final Thoughts

There's no universally "correct" choice between equipment financing and leasing‚Äîit depends entirely on your business circumstances, equipment type, and financial goals. Many successful Canadian businesses use a hybrid approach: financing equipment with long lifespans (buildings, major machinery) while leasing rapidly-changing technology.

Whatever you choose, make sure you:

  • Read all contract terms carefully, especially penalties and fees
  • Get multiple quotes from different providers
  • Have your accountant review the tax implications
  • Consider your 5-year business plan, not just today's needs
  • Calculate total cost of ownership, not just monthly payments

Armed with this knowledge, you're ready to make an informed decision that supports your business growth and financial health.
