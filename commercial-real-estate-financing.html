Commercial Real Estate Financing: Everything You Need to Know

January 31, 202611 min read

The CRE Landscape in 2026

Commercial real estate financing continues to evolve. With interest rates stabilizing and demand growing across sectors, 2026 presents significant opportunities for business owners looking to buy, refinance, or develop commercial properties.

Whether purchasing your first office space, expanding a retail portfolio, or developing industrial property, understanding the financing landscape is essential to making smart decisions.

Types of Commercial Real Estate Loans

Conventional Commercial Mortgages

Standard loans from banks and credit unions for stabilized commercial properties. Typical terms: 5-25 year amortization, 65-80% LTV, fixed or variable rates. Best for established businesses buying owner-occupied property.

SBA 504 Loans

Government-backed loans specifically for owner-occupied commercial real estate. Up to 90% financing (only 10% down), 25-year fixed-rate terms, and competitive rates. Maximum: $5M for most projects, $5.5M for manufacturing.

SBA 7(a) Loans

More flexible than 504 loans — can be used for acquisition, refinancing, renovations, or construction. Up to $5M with 25-year terms for real estate. Slightly higher rates than 504 but more versatile.

CMBS Loans

Pooled loans sold as securities to investors. Larger loan amounts ($2M+), competitive rates, but less flexibility — strict prepayment penalties and limited ability to modify terms.

Bridge Loans

Short-term financing (6-36 months) for properties that need renovation or stabilization before qualifying for permanent financing. Higher rates but fast closing (1-3 weeks).

Construction Loans

Interest-only loans disbursed in stages as construction milestones are met. Typically 12-36 month terms with conversion to permanent financing upon completion.

Down Payment Requirements

  • SBA 504: As low as 10% down
  • SBA 7(a): 10-20% down
  • Conventional: 20-35% down
  • CMBS: 25-35% down
  • Bridge: 20-40% down
  • Construction: 20-30% of total project cost

The most common path for small businesses is the SBA 504 loan, which offers the lowest down payment while providing fixed-rate, long-term financing.

What Lenders Evaluate

Property Metrics

  • DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio): Net operating income divided by annual debt payments. Minimum 1.25x required, 1.4x+ preferred.
  • LTV (Loan-to-Value): Loan amount divided by property value. Most lenders cap at 75-80%.
  • Cap Rate: NOI divided by property value. Indicates investment return.
  • Occupancy: For income properties, lenders want 85%+ occupancy with stable tenants.

Borrower Qualifications

  • Strong personal credit (680+ for most lenders)
  • Real estate experience (especially for larger projects)
  • Liquidity reserves (6-12 months of debt service)
  • Net worth at least equal to the loan amount
  • Business financials showing ability to support the property

Tips for First-Time CRE Buyers

  1. Start with owner-occupied: SBA loans offer the best terms, and using the property for your own business simplifies underwriting.
  2. Get pre-qualified before shopping: Know your budget before you fall in love with a property. This also shows sellers you are serious.
  3. Build your team early: Commercial broker, attorney, inspector, and accountant — all specializing in commercial real estate.
  4. Understand total costs: Beyond the purchase price, budget for closing costs (2-5%), renovations, environmental assessments (Phase I/II), and operating reserves.
  5. Consider the exit: How will you sell or refinance in 5-10 years? Prepayment penalties, loan maturity, and market conditions all matter.
  6. Location analysis: Zoning, traffic patterns, neighboring developments, and demographic trends all impact long-term property value.
